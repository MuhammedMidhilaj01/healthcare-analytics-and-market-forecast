### Cardiovascular Disease Risk Analysis & Healthcare Market Forecast

## Project Overview

This project analyzes cardiovascular disease risk factors using patient health data and explores healthcare market trends using World Bank healthcare expenditure data.

The analysis combines Python for data cleaning, Excel for statistical analysis and forecasting, and Power BI for interactive data visualization.

The objective is to identify major cardiovascular risk factors and evaluate healthcare spending trends to understand future market opportunities in the healthcare sector.

---

## Tools & Technologies

* Python (Pandas, NumPy) – Data cleaning and preprocessing
* Microsoft Excel        – Data analysis, pivot tables, trend analysis, forecasting
* Power BI               – Interactive dashboard visualization

---

## Project Workflow

### 1. Data Cleaning (Python)

* Loaded cardiovascular dataset
* Removed duplicates
* Converted age from days to years
* Created BMI feature
* Generated age group categories

 2. Risk Factor Analysis (Excel)

Pivot table analysis was used to evaluate the relationship between cardiovascular disease and major risk factors:

- Age
- Cholesterol levels
- BMI categories
- Smoking status
- Physical activity

3. Market Trend Analysis (Excel)

Healthcare expenditure data from the World Bank was analyzed to evaluate healthcare market trends.

- Trend analysis from 2000–2023
- CAGR calculation
- Forecasting healthcare spending until 2030

 4. Dashboard Visualization (Power BI)

An interactive dashboard was created to visualize:

- Heart disease risk by age group
- Heart disease risk by cholesterol level
- Heart disease risk by BMI category
- Heart disease risk by smoking
- Healthcare spending trend
- Healthcare spending forecast

---

## Key Insights

- Cardiovascular disease prevalence increases significantly with age.
- High cholesterol levels are strongly associated with increased heart disease risk.
- Healthcare spending as a percentage of GDP in India shows a gradual decline from around 4.1% in the   early 2000s to approximately 3.3% in recent years
- Overweight and obese individuals have a higher probability of cardiovascular disease.
- Forecast analysis suggests a slight downward trend in healthcare spending through 2030, potentially                  stabilizing around 3.0% of GDP
- Rising disease prevalence suggests continued demand for healthcare technologies and medical devices.
