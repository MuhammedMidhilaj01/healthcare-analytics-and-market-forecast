Cardiovascular Risk Insights

## Age Impact

Cardiovascular disease risk increases significantly with age.

Individuals aged 60+ show the highest disease prevalence (~69%), indicating aging as a major cardiovascular risk factor.

## Cholesterol Impact

Patients with high cholesterol levels show substantially higher disease rates (~76%).

This confirms cholesterol as a key clinical predictor for cardiovascular conditions.

## BMI  Impact

Overweight and obese individuals demonstrate a higher probability of heart disease compared to individuals with normal BMI.

Obesity appears to be a strong lifestyle-related risk factor.

## Smoking Impact

Smokers show a slightly higher heart disease rate compared to non-smokers.

This suggests smoking contributes to cardiovascular risk accumulation.

## Healthcare Market Insights

Healthcare Spending Trend

Healthcare spending as a percentage of GDP in India shows a gradual decline from around 4.1% in the early 2000s to approximately 3.3% in recent years

## Market Forecast

Forecast analysis suggests a slight downward trend in healthcare spending through 2030, potentially stabilizing around 3.0% of GDP.

Industry Implication

With rising cardiovascular disease prevalence and stable healthcare spending trends, demand for cardiovascular diagnostics, treatments, and medical devices is expected to continue growing.
